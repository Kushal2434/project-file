Global Heatwave Breaks Temperature Records

By Global Editor | July 19, 2025

A powerful heatwave is gripping the planet, breaking temperature records and putting millions at risk.

China saw highs over 46°C, with electricity demand hitting all-time records as people turned to cooling systems.

Europe had its hottest June ever, leading to 2,300+ heat-related deaths, especially in Spain, the UK, and Portugal.

India and Pakistan endured temperatures up to 48°C, with hundreds of fatalities reported.

Even Iceland and Greenland faced unusually high temperatures, up to 13°C above normal.

Scientists say this extreme heat is driven by climate change, with rising greenhouse gas levels intensifying and prolonging heatwaves. Authorities worldwide are implementing emergency responses like cooling shelters, school closures, and energy warnings, as experts warn that such heat could become the “new normal” if emissions aren't cut.

Japan Develops Earthquake-Resistant Floating Cities

By Ramesh Thapa | July 19, 2025

Japan is making headlines with its innovative approach to earthquake resilience—combining real-world technology and futuristic concepts to prepare for seismic and climate threats.

🏠 Floating Homes with Air Danshin Technology Japanese company Air Danshin Systems has successfully developed earthquake-resistant homes that float during seismic activity. When sensors detect tremors, the system instantly inflates airbags beneath the home, lifting it a few centimeters off the ground. This prevents the building from shaking and significantly reduces damage.

Over 90 homes in Japan have already been fitted with this technology.

It costs around ¥3 million (≈$37,000)—much cheaper than traditional seismic isolation systems.

Shake-table tests have shown the homes remain almost completely stable during earthquakes.

🌊 Vision of Floating Cities On a grander scale, Japanese design firm N-Ark has proposed a futuristic floating city called Dogen City. Designed to house 10,000 residents, the city would include hospitals, schools, green energy systems, and even space-launch facilities—all built to withstand sea-level rise, tsunamis, and natural disasters.

However, Dogen City remains conceptual—with no confirmed start date or budget yet in place.

⚙️ Emerging Tech: Magnetic Earthquake Dampers Japanese researchers are also exploring maglev-based dampers that use magnetic levitation to reduce building sway during earthquakes. While promising, this technology is still in the research stage and faces engineering and cost challenges.

✅ Why It Matters Japan is taking both practical and visionary steps to protect its population from disasters. While floating cities are still on the drawing board, air-lift home foundations are already saving lives, offering new hope for quake-prone areas worldwide.

Significant Oil Reserves Discovered in Nepal

By Ramesh Thapa | July 19, 2025

Geologists have announced a major discovery of oil reserves in the Terai region of Nepal. This find could significantly boost the country’s energy independence and economic growth in the coming years.

The discovery was made after several months of extensive surveying and drilling. Local authorities are hopeful that this could reduce Nepal’s reliance on imported energy resources.

Environmental experts, however, urge caution and call for sustainable and responsible extraction practices to protect the region's biodiversity.
